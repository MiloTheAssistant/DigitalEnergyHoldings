## 1. Executive Vision

Digital Energy Holdings is the parent and shared-infrastructure company for the Digital Energy operating ecosystem.

The website should introduce DEH as the company that centralizes the assets, systems, digital rights, brand infrastructure, intellectual property, contracts, and controlled digital asset treasury strategy behind a growing portfolio of operating companies and brands.

The site should not feel like a retail storefront, marketing agency site, investment pitch deck, crypto promotion, or generic holding-company placeholder. It should feel like a disciplined operating architecture: part corporate home base, part IP vault, part shared-services command center, part digital infrastructure company.

### Public-Facing One-Liner

> Digital Energy Holdings owns and operates the intellectual property, digital rights, brand systems, contracts, technology infrastructure, and shared operating rails behind a portfolio of modern businesses.

### Internal Positioning

Digital Energy Holdings should be treated as the asset-owning and shared-services company for the Digital Energy ecosystem. It owns or controls intellectual property, trademarks, domains, websites, AI systems, marketing frameworks, digital asset libraries, digital rights, contract frameworks, and treasury-governed digital assets. It also provides management, technology, marketing, AI systems, and administrative services to operating companies.

Digital Energy Holdings is not the final top-level umbrella website. For future reference, **Scott Holdings Corp** is the top-level umbrella holding company website and governance layer. DEH should be positioned as the Digital Energy layer that sits below Scott Holdings Corp and above operating companies, brands, rights, assets, contracts, and shared services.

### Strategic Purpose

The site should serve six jobs:

1. Establish Digital Energy Holdings as the credible corporate home for the Digital Energy ecosystem.
2. Explain what DEH owns and controls without exposing private legal, tax, treasury, or banking details.
3. Give bankers, CPAs, vendors, partners, and future counterparties a clean explanation of the operating structure.
4. Create a public anchor for domains, brands, IP, AI systems, digital rights, and operating-company support.
5. Distinguish DEH from Scott Holdings Corp, which will become the top-level umbrella holding company site.
6. Provide a clean foundation for phased Vercel development in the `DigitalEnergyHoldings` GitHub repo.

### Operating Structure Inputs

- **Scott Holdings Corp** is the top-level umbrella holding company and long-term governance/capital allocation layer.
- **Digital Energy Holdings, LLC** is the Digital Energy parent, asset-owning company, and shared-services hub.
- Digital Energy Holdings owns or controls IP, trademarks, brands, domains, websites, AI systems, marketing frameworks, digital rights, DAM libraries, and contract frameworks.
- Digital Energy Holdings provides management, technology, marketing, AI systems, and administrative services.
- Operating companies generate customer-facing revenue and may license brands, systems, IP, and rights from Digital Energy Holdings.
- IP and strategic digital assets should remain centralized in Digital Energy Holdings unless legal counsel documents another structure.
- Credit and shared expenses should be centralized in Digital Energy Holdings, not in Scott Holdings Corp.
- Digital asset treasury activities should remain governed, documented, and separated from personal funds or operating-company funds.

### Governance and Brand Rules

The public website should be simple and confident, but the internal build should respect these guardrails:

- Do not imply that Scott Holdings Corp is an operating business.
- Do not imply that Digital Energy Holdings is a public investment product, fund, custodian, broker, bank, exchange, or investment advisor.
- Do not publish EINs, personal addresses, private treasury counterparties, private custody details, contribution records, wallet details, or tax records.
- Do not over-explain asset protection strategy, internal licensing mechanics, or tax structure.
- Do not frame trademarks, brand names, or trade names as standalone contract parties.
- Do not show operating companies as IP owners unless that has been legally changed.
- Keep public copy focused on ownership, infrastructure, operating support, governance, and portfolio clarity.
- Keep detailed legal ownership language reserved for footer, terms, privacy, contract flows, and private partner documentation.

### Website-Specific Interpretation

Digital Energy Holdings can be presented publicly as:

> A digital asset, intellectual property, and shared-services holding company supporting a portfolio of operating companies, brands, and technology-enabled business systems.

This phrasing should be reviewed before publication, but it aligns with the current project documents showing Digital Energy Holdings as the owner/operator of IP, websites, domains, AI systems, digital assets, marketing systems, and brand frameworks.

## 19. Public Copy Guardrails

### Safe Public Claims

- `Digital Energy Holdings centralizes intellectual property, digital rights, brand systems, domains, contracts, and shared infrastructure.`
- `DEH provides management, technology, marketing, AI systems, and administrative services to affiliated operating companies.`
- `Operating companies can use licensed brands, systems, and digital assets from DEH.`
- `Company-owned digital assets are governed through documented policies and controls.`
- `DEH is part of a disciplined operating-company structure.`
- `Scott Holdings Corp is the top-level umbrella holding company, if this relationship is ready for public display.`

### Claims to Avoid

- `Guaranteed returns.`
- `Invest in DEH.`
- `Bitcoin-backed yield.`
- `We custody assets for others.`
- `We are a bank, broker, exchange, fund, or investment advisor.`
- `We protect assets from creditors.`
- `We reduce taxes through this structure.`
- `Scott Holdings Corp is the operating company.`
- `Operating companies own the IP.`
- `Contracts can be signed by brand names alone.`

### Privacy and Compliance

- Never publish EIN notices, tax notices, personal addresses, private tax details, bank documents, custody account details, wallet details, contribution records, or transaction IDs.
- Keep private Bitcoin treasury controls off the public landing page unless a later compliance-reviewed page is intentionally created.
- Keep the public site focused on company identity, assets, rights, shared services, governance, and contact routing.
- Add disclaimers anywhere digital assets are discussed.

## 20. Internal Entity and IP Notes

These notes are for the build team and should not be displayed as marketing copy unless legal wording is approved.

1. Digital Energy Holdings should remain the owner of website content, domains, design assets, brand systems, AI workflows, digital rights, and IP unless legal counsel documents a different structure.
2. Scott Holdings Corp is the future top-level umbrella holding company website and should not be presented as the operating face of DEH.
3. Digital Energy Media should be treated as a brand or operating property supported by DEH unless a separate legal entity is formed.
4. Scott AI Solutions should be treated according to trademark and brand filing status before public trademark notation is used.
5. Any service agreement should be executed by the correct legal entity.
6. Operating companies should license or receive services from DEH rather than owning core IP directly.
7. Public website copy should preserve a clean boundary between public company information and internal holding-company mechanics.
8. Digital asset treasury references should remain informational and non-promotional.
9. Do not publish private treasury, custody, wallet, bank, or tax details.
